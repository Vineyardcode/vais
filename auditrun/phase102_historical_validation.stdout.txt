  CRITICAL CAVEATS:
    1. 'Italian/Latin best fit' may be a corpus artifact — our Italian
       corpus is small (24K words), and Latin corpora are from wrong era.
    2. Genre fingerprinting is unreliable — recipe texts scored WORSE than
       non-recipe in Phase 76's TTR test. The 'pharmaceutical' conclusion
       rests more on illustrations than on statistical text analysis.
    3. No phase has produced READABLE TEXT. All 'fit' measures are
       distributional, not semantic. Without decipherment, the language
       identification remains circumstantial.
    4. The Bayesian matrix is qualitative. Assigning numeric weights
       to historical evidence is inherently subjective. Different weight
       schemes could change the ranking.
    5. Run-to-run instability (Phase 100) means stochastic methods
       produce unreliable rankings. Only cross-validated, stable results
       should be trusted.
