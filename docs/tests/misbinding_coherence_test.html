Codicological Coherence: Testing the Misbinding Hypothesis
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

External evidence (Lisa Fagin Davis, Manuscript Studies 2020; blog
"Voynich Codicology" Jan 2025) establishes:
 - 5 scribes wrote the VMS
 - Scribes 1 and 2 dominate the botanical section (≈Currier A/B)
 - Scribal work is organized by bifolium, not sequentially
 - This "utterly atypical" pattern implies bifolia were MISBOUND
 - A catastrophic water spill → disbinding → rebinding in wrong order
 - Misbinding occurred early (before 1600s foliation, after quiremarks)

Our question: Can we DETECT the misbinding from the text statistics
alone, and how does it affect our earlier findings?

Tests:
 1. VOCABULARY JACCARD between consecutive folios
    → misbound folios should show lower adjacent-folio similarity
 2. CROSS-FOLIO BIGRAM MI
    → if folio N's last word and folio N+1's first word are from different
      original contexts, their MI should drop relative to within-folio MI
 3. CHARACTER-FREQUENCY CLUSTERING
    → cluster folios by char-freq vectors, check if clusters interleave
      within quires (as Davis found for scribes)
 4. LANGUAGE A/B DETECTION from statistics alone
    → use known statistical differences to classify folios, check
      alignment with quire/bifolium structure
 5. SECTION-MI SENSITIVITY to misbinding
    → re-run word_functional_anatomy's section-MI on randomly permuted botanical folios

SKEPTICAL NOTES:
 - We do NOT have Davis's complete scribe assignments (some published
   in her 2020 paper & 2022 keynote, but not all are public)
 - Currier's A/B is the best-documented linguistic proxy for scribes
 - Currier assignments compiled from voynich.nu, but our mapping may
   have errors — treat as approximate
 - Statistical clustering is unsupervised and might not align with
   paleographic judgments
 - Small-folio word counts create noisy per-folio statistics

Run it in your browser

Python + numpy on WebAssembly, entirely on your machine — nothing is uploaded. First run downloads the runtime (~15 MB) and the repository data pack (~9 MB); both are cached afterwards. Expect it to be slower than a native run.




Run it locally (full speed, byte-exact)


git clone https://github.com/Vineyardcode/vais
cd vais
pip install numpy flask
python scripts/misbinding_coherence_test.py        # this test; output also lands in results/
python webui/server.py          # or the full interactive UI at localhost:5000

ADJUDICATION: Critical Assessment
======================================================================

1. MISBINDING DETECTION FROM TEXT STATISTICS:

   CAUTION: Cross-folio MI is 1.57x HIGHER than within-folio!
   N_cross=199 vs N_within=36059: small-sample MI BIAS inflates
   cross-folio values. This does NOT mean cross-folio bigrams
   are more informative — it is a well-known estimation artifact.
   → Bigram MI test is INCONCLUSIVE for misbinding detection.

2. SCRIBE/LANGUAGE DETECTION:

   Char-frequency clustering agrees only 69% with Currier A/B.
   → Statistical detection is weak (but A/B may be oversimplified)

3. SECTION-MI ROBUSTNESS:

   With 20% botanical folios perturbed: MI retains 93% of baseline
   With 30% botanical folios perturbed: MI retains 90% of baseline
   → word_functional_anatomy section-MI signals are robust to moderate misbinding

4. LANGUAGE A vs B DISTINCTION:

   MI(final_char, language_type) z = 41.0
   → Word-finals STRONGLY distinguish A from B
   → This REINFORCES word_functional_anatomy: finals are grammatical,
     and different scribes may use different grammatical forms

5. IMPLICATIONS FOR OVERALL ANALYSIS:

   a) If bifolia are misbound within quires, our section assignments
      (herbal/astro/bio etc.) are MOSTLY UNAFFECTED because sections
      are defined by illustration content, not folio sequence.

   b) The misbinding DOES affect sequential ordering within sections,
      meaning cross-folio word bigrams may be disrupted. This could
      UNDERESTIMATE true inter-page coherence.

   c) The existence of two distinct writing styles (A/B) that correlate
      with statistical differences is STRONG EVIDENCE against random/
      meaningless text. A hoaxer would produce one style, not two
      consistently different ones organized by bifolium.

   d) word_functional_anatomy's grammatical-finals finding is STRENGTHENED by
      misbinding evidence: if finals vary by scribe/language, they
      are even more likely grammatical (different dialects or scribal
      conventions for the same morphological function).

REVISED CONFIDENCES:
  Natural language favored:    75% (up from 70%)
    Reason: multi-scribe production + consistent grammar + two
    languages organized by bifolium = strong NL signature
  Verbose encoding possible:   65% (down from 70%)
    Reason: multi-scribe verbose encoding is more complex to
    maintain consistently across 5 scribes
  Pure terminators:            <5% (unchanged)
  Random/hoax:                 <5% (down from ~10%)
    Reason: two consistent language types + bifolium organization
    makes hoax extremely unlikely
